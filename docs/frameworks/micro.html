MICRO Framework

Rhythm-based communication layer enabling fluid inter-service coordination at scale.

Protocol Layer Stable v3.1 Low Latency

What is the MICRO Framework?

The MICRO Framework is SIRVATO OS's rhythm-based inter-service communication protocol. Unlike traditional request-response messaging, MICRO operates on synchronized pulse cycles — each service broadcasts its state at a defined rhythm, and the network responds in coordinated harmony.

This approach eliminates polling overhead, reduces thundering-herd problems, and enables predictable low-latency coordination across thousands of microservices simultaneously. Services that fall out of sync are automatically re-entrained within a single pulse window.

Rhythm Principle

Communication flows like music — each service maintains its beat, the conductor (MICRO) harmonizes signals, and the result is a coherent, high-throughput system with zero synchronization overhead.

The Five-Stage Pipeline

Stage 1
📡
Signal

Services emit structured signals at their configured pulse rate. Each signal contains state delta, health metrics, and downstream dependencies.

Stage 2
🥁
Rhythm

The MICRO conductor normalizes pulse frequencies across services, establishing a global beat clock that all participants synchronize to.

Stage 3
🔗
Coordination

Cross-service dependencies are resolved within the pulse window. Resource contention is handled via priority-weighted coordination slots.

Stage 4
🔊
Echo

Confirmation signals propagate back through the network, validating successful coordination and triggering cascading updates across dependents.

Stage 5
🔁
Re-entrainment

Desynchronized services are detected within one pulse cycle and re-entrained to the global rhythm without disrupting in-flight operations.
