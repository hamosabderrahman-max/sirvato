MICRO Core

M-I²-C-R-O — The Foundation of All Communication

The MICRO Core is the base layer of the entire SIRVATO OS architecture. Every other system, interface, and framework builds upon the 5-component MICRO foundation. Master the Core, and you master the system.

Overview

What is MICRO Core?

MICRO Core is the fundamental communication architecture of SIRVATO OS. Before frameworks, before interfaces, before AI Trigger Protocols — there is MICRO. The five-component structure M-I²-C-R-O represents a complete theory of human communication translated into a precision prompt engineering primitive. Every meaningful communication act can be decomposed into these five elements: what you are saying, why you are saying it (and why beneath that), the environment in which you are saying it, the energy with which you are saying it, and the form in which it should be received.

Unlike the ICDF Engine, which structures prompts for AI processing clarity, MICRO Core structures communication itself. This is the distinction that makes MICRO so powerful and so transferable. An ICDF prompt tells an AI what to do. A MICRO prompt communicates a human intent with all the depth and nuance of real communication — and AI systems, trained on vast amounts of human communication, respond to MICRO prompts with correspondingly higher quality and precision.

Component Architecture

The 5 MICRO Components

M
Message The fundamental unit of communication. The "what" you are saying — stripped to its essence. The Message component demands radical clarity: if you cannot state your message in one sentence, you do not yet know what you are trying to say. Everything else in the MICRO structure serves the Message. It is the anchor point against which every other component is calibrated. A strong Message is specific, active, and unambiguous. It does not hedge, qualify, or elaborate — those functions belong to other components.
I²
Intention² Dual-layer intent — the stated goal and the strategic objective beneath it. The first I is the surface intention: what you want the communication to accomplish explicitly. The second I is the meta-intention: what you want the communication to accomplish in the broader strategic context. Example: I¹ = "Explain how our API works." I² = "Make the reader feel competent enough to begin immediately, removing the psychological barrier to adoption." The gap between I¹ and I² is where communication strategy lives.
C
Context Environmental, situational, relational, and temporal context. Context is not just "who is the audience" — it is the entire field in which the communication will be received and interpreted. It includes the platform (where will this be read?), the relational dynamic (how does the recipient relate to the sender?), the emotional state (what is the recipient feeling when they encounter this?), and the temporal position (what just happened? what is about to happen?). Rich context enables precision calibration across all other MICRO components.
R
Rhythm The energy, cadence, tone, and pacing of the exchange. Rhythm is the most underestimated component in prompt engineering. The same Message delivered at different Rhythms produces qualitatively different responses from AI systems — because the training data contains the full spectrum of human tone, and AI models are sensitive to tonal signals in ways that match how humans are sensitive to tone in conversation. High energy rhythm produces momentum-generating output. Measured analytical rhythm produces structured reasoning. Empathic rhythm produces emotionally calibrated communication.
O
Output The delivery specification — format, structure, length, medium, and sequencing of the response. Output is the final MICRO component and the one most often underspecified in conventional prompting. Without a precise Output specification, the AI makes arbitrary choices about format, length, and structure — choices that may technically answer the Message but fail to serve the Intention. A precise Output component transforms a prompt from a question into a complete communication brief: the AI knows not just what to say, but how, in what form, at what length, and in what order.

Core Properties

What Makes MICRO Core Unique

✦

Universal Applicability

MICRO Core applies to every communication task — marketing copy, technical documentation, strategic briefs, code comments, customer emails, API prompts. The 5-component structure is domain-agnostic because it addresses the fundamental structure of communication itself, not the conventions of any particular type of writing or any particular AI model.

✦

Foundation for All Other Frameworks

Every SIRVATO OS framework incorporates MICRO Core components. ICDF's Context field maps to MICRO's C. RCR-EOC's Role informs MICRO's I². AUTO-FLOW's step sequencing operates on MICRO's R (Rhythm) axis. The Unified Mode synthesises all frameworks through the MICRO lens. Understanding MICRO Core deeply is the prerequisite for using every other part of the SIRVATO OS stack at full effectiveness.

✦

Dual-Layer Intention Depth

The I² (Intention-squared) component is MICRO's most distinctive feature. No other prompt engineering framework explicitly models the gap between stated intent and strategic intent. This dual-layer structure is what allows MICRO-structured prompts to produce AI outputs that serve not just the literal request but the underlying goal — which is almost always what the user actually needs, even if they haven't fully articulated it to themselves.

✦

Rhythm as a First-Class Signal

MICRO is the only SIRVATO OS framework that elevates Rhythm to a primary component. This reflects a core insight about how AI models generate text: they are exquisitely sensitive to the tonal fingerprint of the input prompt, and they extend and amplify that fingerprint in their output. By explicitly specifying Rhythm, MICRO users gain direct control over the tone, energy, and cadence of AI-generated content — a lever that most prompting approaches ignore entirely.

Usage Example

MICRO Core Applied

The following demonstrates a fully articulated MICRO Core structure for a content marketing task. Notice how each component is a complete, standalone specification — and how the sum produces a prompt that is qualitatively richer than any conventional single-instruction prompt:

MICRO Core — Complete 5-Component Prompt 
M:  Write a LinkedIn article about why prompt engineering is now a core
    business skill, not just a developer skill.

I²: [Stated] Educate business professionals about prompt engineering value.
    [Strategic] Position our brand as the authority on professional-grade
    prompt engineering — making readers feel they need our tools to be
    competitive in their role.

C:  LinkedIn audience: mid-to-senior business professionals (Directors,
    VPs, C-suite), primarily in tech-adjacent industries. They are
    skeptical of AI hype but open to practical value arguments.
    Publishing on a company page with 12k followers.

R:  Authoritative but accessible. No academic language. Use concrete
    examples from recognisable business contexts. Confident and direct —
    the tone of a trusted expert colleague, not a vendor pitch.

O:  Long-form LinkedIn article, 800–1000 words. Structure: hook (2 lines),
    3 key argument sections with subheadings, practical takeaway section,
    clear call to action at end. No bullet-heavy formatting — flowing
    paragraphs with occasional bold for emphasis.
